Introduction

When it comes to the academic knowledge and discipline in the studies of Economics, “ People often think of economics as concerned only with money, business, and supply and demand. But economics is about much more and has been since its beginnings as a branch of philosophy. The 19th century economist Alfred Marshall said it best when he described economics as the study of people in the business of everyday life. “ (Hall). Economics studies how societies should manage their scarce resources to satisfy people’s wants and needs. This can apply to individuals, families, companies, and even entire nations. Society cannot provide every single thing that people want, so decisions must be made about what should be produced in society to help satisfy those needs, be deciding who should produce, how much, and for who.
