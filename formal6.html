Analysis of Trade Publication: "The study of demand for media products requires an understanding of audience members' preferences, which are shaped by their taste for content," scientists writing in the Journal of Media Economics report. "Despite the central role of content in understanding some aspects of media economics, media economics scholars sometimes apply content analysis in ways that are inconsistent with the generally accepted practices of the method. This article deals with some basic concepts underlying the method of content analysis to familiarize media economics scholars with the method," wrote F.G. Fico and colleagues, Michigan State University. The researchers concluded: "The adoption of accepted content analysis practices will yield better data and, in the long-run, help advance the understanding of media economics." Fico and colleagues published their study in the Journal of Media Economics (A content analysis guide for media economics scholars. Journal of Media Economics, 2008;21(2):114-130). Additional information can be obtained by contacting S. Lacy, Michigan State University, Dept. of Communicable, School Journalism, East Lansing, MI 48824, USA.
